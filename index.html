About

I am a researcher at OpenAI working on safe and ethical AI under Lilian Weng. Previously, I worked at Microsoft Research New England (Cambridge, MA) from its founding in 2008 to 2023 on various fun problems including Code Generation (getting computers to write programs), Fairness, Algorithms, Translating Whale Communication, Game Theory, Computer Humor, Crowdsourcing, and many other things. Before that, I served as an Assistant Professor in Computer Science at Georgia Tech and TTI-Chicago.

To see what I’ve been up to recently, watch a video of some work on Language Models presented at DIMACS Workshop on Foundation Models, Large Language Models, and Game Theory in October, 2023. Also, see an information-theoretic explanation of why LLMs hallucinate.

I’ve chaired and co-chaired a number of conferences and meetings including NEML (New England Machine Learning Day), HCOMP (Conference on Human Computation and Crowdsourcing), and COLT (Conference on Learning Theory).

Short Biography

Adam Tauman Kalai is a Member of Technical Staff and Research Scientist at OpenAI working on AI Safety and Ethics. He is quite old and has worked in multiple fields, including Algorithms, Machine Learning Theory, Game Theory, and Crowdsourcing. He received his BA from Harvard and PhD from Carnegie Mellon University. He has served as an Assistant Professor at Georgia Tech and the Toyota Technological Institute at Chicago. He is a member of the science team of Project CETI, an interdisciplinary initiative to understand the communication of sperm whales. He has co-chaired AI and crowdsourcing conferences including COLT (the Conference on Learning Theory), HCOMP (the Conference on Human Computation) and NEML. His honors include the Majulook prize, best paper awards, an NSF CAREER award, and an Alfred P. Sloan fellowship.
